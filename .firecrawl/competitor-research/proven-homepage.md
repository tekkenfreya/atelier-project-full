#### What’s the difference between chemical and physical sunscreen?

SPF is a crucial step in any skincare routine, as sun exposure can accelerate the look of fine lines and wrinkles and increase skin cancer risk. Both chemical and physical sunscreens provide broad-spectrum UVA/UVB protection but differ in how they block the sun’s harmful rays. Chemical sunscreens utilize FDA-approved ingredients such as octocrylene, avobenzone, and homosalate to sink into the skin and absorb sun rays, turning the rays into heat and releasing them from the skin. Physical sunscreen is composed of mineral ingredients such as zinc oxide and/or titanium dioxide that act as a protective layer on your skin, deflecting sun rays.

At PROVEN, we only formulate physical sunscreens or hybrid sunscreens that use a mix of chemical and physical sun protection. Chemical sunscreens pose some environmental risks and are known to cause reactions in people with sensitive skin.

#### How do I maximize product absorption on my skin?

When using new products for the first time, many customers commonly experience skin purging. The powerful active ingredients in your PROVEN Skincare system may help to accelerate skin cell turnover– the process in which the skin starts shedding dead skin cells faster than expected. New, fresh skin cells make their way to the surface, pushing out any existing buildup and excess sebum. Purging looks different from person to person and can be a combination of whiteheads, blackheads, cysts, papules, and pustules. It typically takes a complete skin cycle (about 28 days) for your skin to adjust and reflect clearer, glowing skin fully. However, some night creams can cause clogged pores, further irritating the skin. Breakouts happen in an area where you usually don't and take longer to disappear or shrink. It's important to know when a night cream is not suitable for your skin.
