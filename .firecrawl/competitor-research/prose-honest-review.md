## 2\. APPLICATION: 4/5

**CLEANSER -**

- CLAIM: a"non-stripping formula"

- FYI I received a gel cleanser, they have a cream cleanser option as well

- This cleanser wasn’t drying or stripping at all for me which I loved

- One pump did the trick and there was solid lathering which made it easy to work into skin

- Overall, pretty simple (I'll talk about how well it cleansed in a second)


**SERUM-**

- CLAIM: "fast-absorbing & dewy formula"

- The serum wasn't stick or heavy

- I’d use about a dime size in my palm, the brand recommends a few drops, but the milky serum came out pretty quickly

- From there it’s pretty easy to pat onto the skin and give it a minute before applying moisturizer on top

- There's sodium hyaluronate inside so I didn't want this on dry skin, I apply on damp skin after cleansing

- Side note: I'm not a huge fan of sodium hyaluronate because it's in everything right now (skincare & makeup). If I were to get the serum again, I'd see if they could customize a formula without it or I'd pass on that step, but I'm getting ahead of myself...


**MOISTURIZER -**

- CLAIM: "fast-absorbing"

- I received the cream moisturizer and the texture was really nice, lightweight, but held on overnight

- There was no greasy feeling after applying and this didn't seem like the type of formula that was going to rub off on pillowcases overnight, but it did hold on (a little goes a long way for me - it’s hot and humid here so that tracks)

## 3\. CLEANSER: 4/5

- $29 (subscription $24.65)

- This is a water-first formula followed by surfactants/emulsifiers, texture enhancers

- From there I see niacinamide (yay) with preservatives and the called out ingredients of lime pearl and mushroom stem extract (also one polymer is in there from what I can see)

- CLAIM: "gentle, non-stripping formula with a low pH to support a healthy skin barrier and preserve skin hydration"

- I love a cleansing balm and oil so when I saw the gel cleanser, I gave it a bit of a side-eye which is completely subjective. That said, the point is to hand over my skincare to experts so here I am...

- Three weeks in, I'm liking it! The brand recommends using this twice a day, I mainly use it at night unless I use my **ZIIP micro/nanocurrent device** in the morning and need to remove that gel

- Did it remove makeup? Yes. Mascara? Yep, but the truth is, I found myself missing the cleansing balms - it’s a texture thing

- Did it leave any residue behind? None at all, and this was a stand-out for me, my skin felt very ‘clean’ and not at all dry

- I am testing this during the summer months while humidity is at level-100000 so I also think, if I were to refine with the next order, I may try the cream cleanser option

- I'll compare my results with their clinical study (below):


  - _“Clinical study results: 90%+ say their cleanser left their skin clean, smooth, soft and clear. Also the cream cleanser didn't leave skin feeling dry. 86% said their cleanser removed oily residue and traces of makeup_

    _\*Based on a 4-week clinical study of 22 people, ages 18 to 65 with sensitive skin, who used Prose Gel or Cream Cleanser twice a day”_

  - Me _:_ my skin felt clean and smooth, not so much soft after using this. It did remove makeup well, so all in all, I was in line with the study

## 4\. SERUM: 3/5

- $64 (subscription $54.40)

- This is also a water-first formula with rose hip seed oil, bakuchiol, a polymer, but also beta glucan (love this), and peptides (also, love this)

- CLAIM: "a highly-concentrated, fast-absorbing & ‘dewy’ formula"

- It's definitely dewy and absorbed relatively quickly, not lightning fast mind you

- I still like to wait a couple of minutes before locking things in with a moisturizer on top, but that’s pretty normal for me

- CLAIM: "clinically proven to hydrate skin, boost radiance and visibly reduce the appearance of fine lines"

- As for boosting radiance and reducing the appearance of fine lines, I haven't seen that in a noticeable way. It may take more time?

- Here’s the clinical study:

  - _“Clinical study results: 90%+ said their skin barrier felt strong and their skin was glow with increased firmness. 82%+ said their complexion evened-out and the appearances of wrinkles as well as blemishes was reduced." \*Based on a 4-week use test of 72 people, ages 25 to 70 with sensitive skin, who used Prose Serum twice a day”_

  - My results _:_ I can't say I saw a stronger barrier (I’m not sure it’s super visible to the naked eye), increased glow or firmness. The bigger issue for me was the evening-out as I saw a bit more redness than normal after three weeks. This is also why I only kept this routine to once-a-day. I also mentioned this, but the appearance of wrinkles wasn't diminished in a significant way so I can keep testing to see if that shifts.
- I almost gave this a ‘2’ for performance, but just because I saw some redness doesn’t mean you will. That ingredients list is packed with some heavy hitters so I’ll keep it at a ‘3’ for now

- I’m going to test the cleanser and cream on their own for the next couple of weeks. My gut tells me the serum may be the ‘miss’ out of the three for me

## 5\. MOISTURIZER: 3/5

- $52 (subscription $44.20)

- Another a water-first formula that includes squalane up front. From there I'm seeing rose hip seed oil and jojoba esters

- One ingredient I'm seeing everywhere out there is 'ectoin' which people claim to be similar to niacinimide, just something interesting to see

- CLAIM: "a nourishing, fast-absorbing face moisturizer that targets your specific skin needs, providing powerful protection against environmental stressors"

- CLAIM: "scientifically proven to provide 8-hour hydration\*

- I found this to absorb pretty quickly and it was hydrating overnight in a non-greasy way

- CLAIM: "boosts skin’s firmness & elasticity"

- I'm not seeing this in a significant way after three weeks

- Overall, I liked the moisturizer most out of the three products - the texture and absorption stood out. It feels like the perfect 'workhorse' moisturizer for summer months. I'd probably need a more dense option for dry, winter months which I could refine with the brand

- As for how my experience compared to the study:

  - _“Clinical study results: 90%+ said skin looked healthy, glowy and felt comfortable. While 83%+ said the moisturizer made skin feel soft, their skin barrier feel stronger and reduced the appearance of fine lines._

    _\*Based on a 4-week use test of 72 people, ages 25 to 70 with sensitive skin, who used Prose Cream or Gel Moisturizer twice a day”_

  - Me: my skin wasn't glowy, but did feel comfortable. As for the rest, I can't say it stood up to those claims just yet. Felt great, but as of week three, I’m not seeing drastic positive shifts on the surface of my skin

### TOTAL SCORE - 14/20

### PROS:

- the level of customization here, if it works for you, could be a game changer

- silicone & alcohol free plus these are vegan and cruelty-free formulas

- you have the ability to customize and refine further (with a great return policy which is great since you can’t really try samples of customized products)

- eco-friendly considerations from component to shipping packaging along with refills (and ideally, if you customize skincare, there's hopefully less waste because you like it)

- fragrance-free options are available

- all products were easy to apply and absorbed quickly as promised

- the pricing is really competitive for what you get here compared to some other lines on the market


### CONS:

- I mentioned this, but keep in mind, my results are after three weeks using this routine once a day compared to clinical study results for those using it twice a day for 4 weeks

- I’m also not convinced another week would help as much as refining the serum would since I think it was causing a bit of redness

- serum performance, when it comes to diminishing fine lines, wasn't stellar

- it could be tricky to commit to the same line over and over, even with variations and refinements available - single product purchases look like they’re available even though the brand recommends the full system for best results (this level of flexibility may have me coming back for that moisturizer)

- there are no silicones, but a couple of polymers inside based on my quick scan - these aren’t dealbreakers, I’m certainly not a cosmetic chemist, but I do personally try to avoid silicones and polymers
