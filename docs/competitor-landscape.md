## Market Segmentation

The personalized skincare market splits into three tiers:

| Tier | Model | Examples | Barrier to Entry |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quiz-Based Custom** | AI/algorithm selects ingredients from a quiz, no prescription | Prose, Proven, Pure Culture, Skinsei | Medium — requires formulation expertise + tech |
| **Prescription Custom** | Licensed provider prescribes active ingredients (tretinoin, azelaic acid) | Agency/Curology, Dermatica, Apostrophe, Ro Custom Rx | High — requires medical licensing, telehealth infra |
| **Partial Customization** | Choose from pre-made modules/concentrates to mix | Clinique iD, Skin Inc., Function of Beauty | Low — essentially product bundling with a UI |

**Kyrill sits in Tier 1: Quiz-Based Custom.** Direct competitors are Prose and Proven. Prescription brands (Tier 2) serve a different customer need (medical-grade treatment) and are not direct threats but compete for wallet share.

| **Clean Claims** | Cruelty-free, dye-free, dermatologist tested, clean/non-toxic ingredients |

#### Proven — Strengths

- **SPF included** in day moisturizer — Prose and Kyrill don't include SPF
- **Day + Night distinction** — separate moisturizers for AM/PM shows sophistication
- **Stanford dermatologist backing** — strong credibility signal
- **Larger ingredient database claim** (20,238 analyzed vs. Prose's 185+ used)
- **3-month subscription cycle** — lower decision frequency than monthly
- **"Reformulation guarantee"** — if it doesn't work, they reformulate for free
- **SkinSpecific line** — simpler option for customers who don't want full customization

#### Proven — Weaknesses

- **Higher base routine cost** ($129.99 for 3 products, but no serum included — serum is $99.99 extra)
- **No serum in core system** — the 3-step is cleanser + day moisturizer + night cream. To match Prose/Kyrill's cleanser-serum-moisturizer lineup, customer pays $129.99 + $99.99 = $229.98
- **3-month subscription lock** — less flexible than monthly/bi-monthly
- **Less brand awareness** than Prose
- **Quiz is only 3 minutes** — may feel superficial despite 47 factors claim

## Adjacent Competitors

These brands use a **prescription model** — a licensed medical provider creates the formula. Different business model but same customer intent (personalized skincare that works).

|---|---|
| **Model** | Prescription custom formula via licensed dermatology provider (NP, PA, MD, DO) |
| **Core Product** | "Future Formula" — custom prescription cream |
| **Additional** | Dark Spot Formula, eye balm, sunscreen, hydrating serum |
| **Pricing** | $80 every 2 months ($40/month), both formulas: $130/2 months ($65/month) |
| **Active Ingredients** | Tretinoin, azelaic acid, tranexamic acid, niacinamide (prescription-grade) |
| **Consultation** | Photos + medical history form, matched with licensed provider |
| **Adjustments** | Provider adjusts formula over time based on check-ins |
| **Clean Claims** | Vegan, cruelty-free, unscented, paraben-free |
| **Cancellation** | "Free, easy cancellation" |
| **Award** | Allure Best of Beauty winner |

#### Agency — Assessment

- **Different model entirely** — prescription ingredients (tretinoin) that Kyrill cannot offer without medical licensing
- **Strongest credibility** — actual dermatology providers, not just an algorithm
- **Higher price** ($40-65/month vs. Kyrill's ~$38/month at subscription price)
- **Competes for the same customer** who wants "skincare that actually works"
- **Key lesson for Kyrill:** Agency's messaging around "a personal dermatology provider" and ongoing check-ins is powerful. Kyrill's quiz and formula refinement need to feel equally expert-backed even without medical licensing

## Key Takeaways for Kyrill

### 1. The Market Has Room

The quiz-based custom skincare tier has only **two serious players**: Prose (haircare-first) and Proven (expensive when you add serum). Neither owns the market for a **skincare-focused, 3-product, quiz-driven routine at $145 with a 20% subscription discount**.

### 2. Pricing Is Right

Kyrill's $145 routine matches Prose exactly and undercuts Proven significantly ($215 with serum). The 20% subscription discount ($116/routine) beats every non-prescription competitor.

### 3. The Quiz Is the Differentiator

Every competitor's quiz is either:
- Too shallow (Prose — "super simple" per reviewers, Proven — 3 minutes)
- Too clinical (Agency — medical history and photos)
- Too superficial (Function of Beauty — skin type + fragrance + color)

Kyrill can own the middle ground: deep enough to feel expert, accessible enough to feel enjoyable, transparent enough to build trust.

### 4. SPF Is a Gap

Proven includes SPF in their day moisturizer. Prose and Kyrill do not. Consider whether adding SPF to the moisturizer (or offering it as an option) would be a competitive advantage.

### 5. Medical Credibility Matters

Proven's Stanford dermatologist backing and Agency's licensed providers set a credibility bar. Kyrill should consider:
- Partnering with a dermatologist as advisor/formulator
- Citing clinical research for ingredient choices
- Running clinical studies (even small ones) for claims

### 6. Formula Lock Is a Unique Feature

No competitor offers a "keep my formula" option. Prose and Proven both auto-evolve formulas, which frustrates some users. Formula lock is a genuine innovation.

### 7. Subscription Transparency Wins

Agency explicitly says "free, easy cancellation." Prose gets complaints about hard-to-cancel subscriptions. Kyrill should make subscription management the simplest in the category.

### 8. Fulfillment Speed Is an Opportunity

If Kyrill can ship in 3-5 days instead of Prose's 7-12, that's a meaningful UX advantage.
