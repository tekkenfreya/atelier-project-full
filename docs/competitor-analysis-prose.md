## Consultation Quiz

### Structure

The quiz is organized into 3 sections:
1. **Lifestyle** — age, location (zip code), diet, stress, sleep, exercise, water intake
2. **My Skin** — skin type, sensitivity, concerns (blemishes, dryness, oiliness, hyperpigmentation, fine lines, redness, dark spots, pores, uneven texture)
3. **Preferences & Goals** — fragrance preference (or fragrance-free), primary skin goals, product selection

### Key Design Decisions

- Quiz starts immediately — no account creation required upfront
- Email collection happens mid-quiz (to save progress and for remarketing)
- Multi-step form with progress indicator (3 sections shown in nav)
- Clean, minimal UI with one question per screen
- Location data (zip code) used to factor in environmental conditions (UV, pollution, humidity, hard water)
- "Resume here" option for returning users

### What the Quiz Determines

Prose claims to examine 85+ factors across:
- Skin type and concerns
- Environmental conditions (climate, pollution, UV, water hardness — tied to zip code)
- Lifestyle factors (diet, stress, exercise, hydration, sleep)
- Personal preferences (fragrance, goals)

The AI algorithm then selects from 185+ clean ingredients to build a personalized formula for each product.

### Formula Personalization Examples (from site)

**Andy** (30, NYC 10013): Dry skin + hyperpigmentation + fine lines. Environment: pollution, strong UV, low humidity. Key ingredients: Bakuchiol, Squalane, Hyaluronic Acid.

**Kassie** (32, Washington DC 20001): Oily skin + hyperpigmentation + fine lines. Environment: low UV, hard water, seasonal humidity. Key ingredients: Niacinamide, Rosehip Oil, Caviar Lime.

This demonstrates real ingredient variation based on quiz answers — not just marketing copy swaps.

### Product Pages (PDP)

Each PDP includes:
1. Product image carousel (4 images)
2. Product subtitle (e.g., "Cleanse + Balance")
3. Product name + description
4. Key claims (3 bullet points)
5. Clinical study footnote
6. Buy once / Subscribe toggle with pricing
7. "Get your formula" CTA (links to quiz)
8. Clinical results stats (percentages)
9. Before/after carousel
10. Sample formula cards (real customer examples with ingredients)
11. FAQ section
12. "Complete your routine" cross-sell

### Review & Refine System

Prose's unique retention tool — after each order, customers provide feedback on product performance. This data feeds back into the algorithm to adjust formulas for the next order. Creates a flywheel: more feedback = better formulas = higher retention.

*Note: Prose counts 5-star reviews per product per order, including repeat reviews from the same customer. This inflates the "500K+ 5-star reviews" headline.*

## Customer Complaints & Weaknesses

### From Thingtesting (4.1/5, 1,035 reviews)

**Negative themes:**
- Inconsistent reformulation performance — formula changes between orders don't always improve
- Drying or residue-prone formulas for some users
- Subscription hard to cancel / skip shipments confusing
- Results sometimes no better than standard brands
- Conditioner specifically criticized as runny/underwhelming

### From GQ Review

**Cons:**
- More expensive than other haircare/skincare lines
- Subscription model feels aggressive

**Pros:**
- Excellent personalization and packaging
- Good fragrance options (including fragrance-free)
- Almost immediate effects from some products

### From Reddit (r/SkincareAddiction)

- Mixed feedback — some users love it, others find it unremarkable
- Price-to-performance questioned by skincare enthusiasts who know ingredients

### From The Styleshaker (Honest Review)

- Products perform well but not dramatically better than competitors
- Quiz is "super simple" — holistic approach appreciated
- Sponsored review, so take positivity with context

### Key Vulnerability Patterns

1. **Price sensitivity:** $145 for routine is high for what are fundamentally generic actives (Niacinamide, Hyaluronic Acid, Vitamin E) in customized concentrations
2. **Reformulation inconsistency:** The "always-evolving formula" is a double-edged sword — customers who liked order #2 may dislike order #3
3. **Subscription friction:** Cancellation/skip process generates complaints
4. **Small clinical studies:** 10-22 subjects is not robust evidence
5. **Skincare is secondary:** Haircare is the core business. Skincare gets less R&D attention and has much fewer reviews
6. **Review inflation:** Counting repeat 5-star reviews from the same customer inflates social proof
7. **Made-to-order shipping:** 7-12 day shipping is slow vs. Amazon-era expectations

## Critical Analysis — What Kyrill Can Exploit

### 1. Deeper Quiz, Better Personalization

Prose examines "85+ factors" but the actual skincare quiz is described as "super simple" by reviewers. There's a gap between the marketing claim and perceived depth. Kyrill can differentiate by building a genuinely deep consultation (25+ questions with visible branching logic) that *feels* comprehensive. The quiz experience itself is part of the product.

### 2. Transparent Ingredient Logic

Prose shows sample formula cards but doesn't explain *why* specific ingredients were chosen. Kyrill can show the reasoning: "Because you live in [city] with hard water and high UV, we selected [ingredient] to [specific benefit]." This builds trust and justifies the premium.

### 3. Formula Stability

Prose's "always-evolving" formulas frustrate users who find a formula they love, only to have it change. Kyrill can offer **formula lock** — let customers choose to keep their current formula or opt into refinement. Best of both worlds.

### 4. Subscription Transparency

Prose's subscription is hard to cancel/modify according to complaints. Kyrill can make subscription management dead simple — one-click skip, pause, cancel, no dark patterns. This becomes a trust differentiator.

### 5. Bigger Subscription Discount

Prose offers 15%. Kyrill plans 20%. That's a meaningful $7.25/routine difference on an identical $145 base price. Lead with this in marketing.

### 6. Faster Fulfillment

Prose's made-to-order model means 7-12 day shipping. If Kyrill can ship faster (even if formulas are simulated-custom from a finite ingredient matrix rather than truly made-to-order), speed becomes a competitive advantage.

### 7. Skincare-First, Not Skincare-Second

Prose is a haircare company that added skincare. Kyrill is skincare-first. This means:
- 100% of R&D focus on 3 products
- No brand dilution across categories
- Marketing can position as specialist vs. generalist

### 8. Clinical Credibility

Prose's clinical studies are weak (10-22 subjects). Kyrill doesn't need to match this — but if Kyrill eventually runs even modest studies with larger sample sizes, it becomes a credibility edge.

### 9. Pricing Strategy

Prose's aggressive first-order discount (50% off) suggests high customer acquisition cost and potentially low first-order margin. Kyrill could consider a smaller first-order discount (20-30%) with better LTV economics, or match the aggression if CAC allows.

### 10. Review Authenticity

Prose inflates review counts by counting repeat reviews from the same customer. Kyrill can build authentic social proof with verified-purchase, one-review-per-order methodology and position this as "real reviews, not inflated metrics."
